An ectopic pregnancy is a pregnancy that implants outside the uterus, most commonly in a fallopian tube, and it can become a medical emergency.

An ectopic pregnancy is when a pregnancy grows in the wrong place, usually outside the womb.

### Instruction:
What are the symptoms of ectopic pregnancy?

### Response:
Symptoms include abdominal or pelvic pain, vaginal bleeding, dizziness, shoulder pain, and in severe cases collapse from internal bleeding.

### Instruction:
What causes ectopic pregnancy?

### Response:
Ectopic pregnancy is associated with damage or blockage of the fallopian tubes, prior pelvic infection, previous ectopic pregnancy, or other risk factors.

### Instruction:
How is ectopic pregnancy treated?

### Response:
Treatment may include medication such as methotrexate or surgery, depending on the location, size, and severity of the condition.

### Instruction:
What is an emergency presentation?

### Response:
An emergency presentation is a set of symptoms or signs that may indicate a serious condition requiring urgent medical assessment.

### Instruction:
Explain emergency presentation in simple terms.

### Response:
An emergency presentation means symptoms are serious enough that urgent medical help may be needed.

### Instruction:
What are the symptoms of an emergency presentation?

### Response:
Symptoms may include chest pain, severe shortness of breath, loss of consciousness, stroke-like symptoms, severe bleeding, or sudden severe pain.

### Instruction:
What causes an emergency presentation?

### Response:
Emergency presentations can be caused by serious conditions such as heart attack, stroke, sepsis, pulmonary embolism, trauma, or internal bleeding.

### Instruction:
How is an emergency presentation treated?

### Response:
Emergency presentations are treated by urgent medical evaluation, stabilization, and condition-specific emergency care.
